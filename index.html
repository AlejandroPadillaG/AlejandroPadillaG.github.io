About Me

I’m Jane Doe, a PhD student in the Department of Computational Mathematics at Example University. My work focuses on high‑order numerical methods for non‑linear partial differential equations arising in climate modelling.

Research Interests

  • High‑order discontinuous Galerkin schemes
  • Reduced‑order modelling and surrogate optimisation
  • GPU acceleration of scientific computing workflows
  • Open‑source scientific software engineering

Selected Publications

  1. Doe, J., Smith, A. & Nguyen, T. Entropy‑stable DG methods for moist‑atmosphere dynamics. SIAM J. Sci. Comput., 2024.
  2. Doe, J. & Chen, L. Graph‑based hyper‑reduction for nonlinear PDEs. J. Computational Physics, 2023.
